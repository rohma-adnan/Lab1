Fun Fact About Galaxies

Most Distant Galaxy:
The most distant galaxy ever discovered is GN-z11, located over 13 billion light-years away. It formed only 400 million years after the Big Bang, making it one of the oldest galaxies we can observe!

Galaxies Collide!:
Our Milky Way galaxy is on a collision course with the Andromeda galaxy. In about 4.5 billion years, the two will collide, merging into a new, larger galaxy. But don't worryâ€”star collisions are unlikely!

Rogue Galaxies:
There are galaxies called "rogue galaxies" that have been ejected from their original clusters and drift freely through space. They have no gravitational ties to any larger structures!

The Largest Galaxy:
The largest galaxy known is IC 1101, which is about 6 million light-years in diameter. That's roughly 60 times the size of the Milky Way!



Types of Galaxies

  1. Spiral Disk-shaped with arms (like the Milky Way)
  2. Elliptical Round or oval, mostly old stars
  3. IrregularNo clear shape, messy and chaotic
  4. Barred Spiral Spiral with a central bar (Milky Way too!)
